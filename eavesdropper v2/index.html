Eavesdropper v2

  • High Concept: Progress incrementally by eavesdropping on the crowd and buying upgrades.
  • Theme: Eavesdropping
  • Mandated Variety: Input: Buttons; Randomness: Deterministic; Play style: Achiever; Genres: Incremental Game (other)
  • Player Experience Goals: I hope they get satisfaction from the constant upgrades and sense of accomplishment.
  • Gameplay: The player waits to see when blue people turn red, which means they can be eavesdropped on. Click on a red person to eavesdrop, and gain a little bit of information about a conversation. Each eavesdrop will net you some money. Once you have enough tidbits, you can sell the conversation for a bonus profit. If you click on a blue person however, your bonus will reset to zero. Use your income to buy upgrades, which in turn allow you to collect money at faster rates.
  • Prototype Goal: Basic idle mechanics: collect a currency and invest that same currency to increase the future rate of income.
  • Features: Basic idle games tend to have an automatic component, and a manual component. My manual "whack-a-mole" mechanic is not something I've personally seen done before in idle games. I've played quite a few, and I still find this fun and interesting. What I improved from the original design:
      Rules/Procedures/Core Mechanic Enhancements:
      • Clicking on red people gives you money, not just selling your bonus.
      • The crowd now moves across the screen, making it more difficult to click the red people.
      • Clicking on a blue person now resets your bonus bar back to zero, to add some difficulty.
      • You cannot click on more red people if your bonus bar is full. You must sell the bonus first.
      Polish Enhancements:
      • Added moving background for scenary.
      • Added additional, meaningful sounds for clicking on blue people and for the bonus being maxed out.
      • Rearranged some UI elements so they work with the game's new design.
  • Setting/Premise: A man is a professional wire-tapper. He needs to collect conversations and sell them to make a living. He likes to enter dense crowds and collect as many tidbits as he can.
  • Story: A basic story for this prototype could simply be that he needs to listen to some conversations to make money.
  • Target Audience: Bored teenagers
  • Play Time: Depending on how active the player is, they could probably finish the game in around 30 minutes.
  • Strategies: There are two main strategies: be very active and manual, or let the game do the work. Most players combine the strategies.
